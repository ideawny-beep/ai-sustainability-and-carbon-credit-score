1. Daily Impact: Dos for Reducing Emissions

✓ Travel Smart
  • **DO** prioritize public transport, cycling, or walking for short commutes.
  • **DO** explore hybrid or electric vehicles when buying a new car.
  • **DO** choose direct flights where possible to reduce time in high-emissions ascent/descent phases.
✓ Power Efficiency
  • **DO** switch to LED lighting and upgrade to Energy Star-rated appliances.
  • **DO** adjust your thermostat by a few degrees—small changes yield big energy savings.
  • **DO** switch your energy provider to one that uses 100% renewable sources (if available).
✓ Sustainable Diet
  • **DO** incorporate more plant-based meals into your weekly routine.
  • **DO** buy locally sourced, seasonal produce to reduce transport emissions.
  • **DO** reduce food waste by planning meals and composting leftovers.

2. Carbon Credits: Dos and Don'ts of Offsetting

The Carbon Credit DOs

  • 1.
    Prioritize Reduction First: **DO** treat credits as a last resort for unavoidable emissions. The most sustainable action is always reducing your footprint.
  • 2.
    Verify the Project: **DO** look for third-party verification from reputable organizations like Gold Standard or Verified Carbon Standard (VCS).
  • 3.
    Focus on Additionality: **DO** ensure the project would not have happened without the carbon credit funding (i.e., it must be "additional").

The Carbon Credit DON'Ts

  • 1.
    DON'T Rely Solely on Credits: **DON'T** use offsetting as a license to continue high-emissions behavior (known as "greenwashing").
  • 2.
    DON'T Ignore Permanence: **DON'T** choose short-term projects (like simply planting trees) without guaranteeing their long-term survival and maintenance.
  • 3.
    DON'T Forget Co-Benefits: **DON'T** choose projects that only focus on $\text{CO}_2$; prioritize those that also support biodiversity or local communities.
